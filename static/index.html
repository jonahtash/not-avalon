Court Scroll: Rules & Roles

The Battle for Camelot

Avalon is a game of social deduction where players are divided into Loyal Servants of Arthur (Good) fighting for honor, and Minions of Mordred (Evil) plotting from the shadows.

1
Lobby & Role Assignment: The Host launches the game, which automatically distributes secret identity cards to all players. Only certain roles know who else is on their side.
2
Propose Teams: A Leader is chosen who proposes a quest team of a specific size (indicated on the Mission Track). All players then vote (Approve/Reject) on this team.
3
Quest Execution: If the team is approved, members vote secretly to "Succeed" or "Fail" the mission. Good players MUST vote Success. Evil players can choose Success or Failure.
4
Winning Conditions:
  • Good Wins: If 3 missions successfully pass AND Merlin survives the final Assassin's phase.
  • Evil Wins: If 3 missions fail OR if the Assassin correctly guesses Merlin's identity at the end of the game.

Merlin (Good)

The wizard Merlin knows who the Evil players are. However, he must speak in riddles. If he is too obvious, the Assassin will kill him at the end, stealing victory for Evil.

Percival (Good)

Percival is Merlin's protector. He sees two players marked as Merlin (Merlin and Morgana), but doesn't know who is who. He must figure out the real Merlin and guide the group according to Merlin's subtle hints.

Loyal Servant of Arthur (Good)

Standard Good character. You do not know anyone else's role. You must pay attention to votes, mission outcomes, and discussions to deduce who is Good.

Assassin (Evil)

Leader of Evil's end-game. If Good succeeds 3 missions, the Assassin makes a final guess on who Merlin is. If correct, Evil wins the game instantly.

Morgana (Evil)

A sorceress who disguises herself. To Percival, Morgana looks exactly like Merlin, creating confusion. Her job is to make Percival trust her instead of the real Merlin.

Mordred (Evil)

The ultimate traitor. Mordred's identity is hidden from Merlin. Merlin does not see Mordred as Evil, making Mordred highly dangerous and easy to sneak into quest teams.

Oberon (Evil)

An isolated rogue. Oberon does not know who the other Evil players are, nor do they know him. However, Merlin does know Oberon is Evil. Oberon must execute chaos blindly.

Special Roles Insight Guide

To ensure players fully understand what information they receive at the start of a match, review these absolute examples:

Playing as Merlin

Suppose players are: Alice (Merlin), Bob (Percival), Charlie (Assassin), David (Morgana), and Eve (Mordred).

What Merlin (Alice) sees:
Charlie is Evil, David is Evil.
Note: Eve (Mordred) is hidden from Merlin and is NOT shown.

Playing as Percival

Suppose players are: Alice (Merlin) and David (Morgana).

What Percival (Bob) sees:
"You see Merlin or Morgana in: Alice, David" (randomly shuffled).
Bob must watch Alice and David's behavior. The one playing logically is Merlin; the one attempting to mislead is Morgana.

Playing as Evil (Assassin / Morgana / Mordred)

Suppose players are: Charlie (Assassin), David (Morgana), Eve (Mordred), and Frank (Oberon).

What the Evil players (Charlie, David, Eve) see:
They see each other's names and roles: David (Morgana) and Eve (Mordred).
Note: Frank (Oberon) is NOT shown to them, as Oberon does not know the coven, nor do they know Oberon.
